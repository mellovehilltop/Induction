📝 The Golden Rules

🚨 Critical: All PPE (except boots) must be kept off the floor. If it touches the floor, discard and replace immediately!
📦 Storage: Personal lockers are for personal items only. PPE is company-issued and must NOT be taken home.

👔 The Right Order - PPE Steps

👔 PPE Dressing Sequence
  • Hairnet & Beard Snood: Put on your hairnet first (and beard snood if needed). You may need two hairnets for longer hair
  • Hat & Footwear: Put on your hat and appropriate boots/shoes
  • Hand Sanitising: Sanitise your hands thoroughly
  • Coat: Put on your coat and fasten all the way up to cover your personal clothing
  • Final Hand Wash: Wash and disinfect your hands again

🏭 Removing PPE Safely

🏭
Leaving Production
Remove all PPE except hats, hairnets, and shoes
🗑️
Dispose Properly
Hairnets must be discarded and not reused
☕

Remove white coat before eating, smoking, or using toilet

🧼 Module 3: Hygiene & Food Safety

Hygiene is the foundation of food safety. Learn the essential practices that protect our honey and customers.

🙌 Hand Washing - Your First Defence

🙌 Proper Hand Washing Technique
  • Wet hands: Use running water
  • Apply soap: Lather thoroughly
  • Scrub everything: Palms, back of hands, thumbs, and between fingers
  • Rinse well: With running water
  • Dry completely: Use paper towels
  • Apply disinfectant: Rub in hand sanitizer

⏰ When to Wash Your Hands

🚨 Critical Times: You MUST wash your hands at these specific times to maintain food safety standards.

🩹 Managing Cuts and Grazes

🚨 Important: All cuts and grazes must be covered with waterproof, blue metal detectable plasters provided by the company.
🩹
Company Plasters Only
Must be applied by authorised person
🧤
Gloves Over Plasters
When handling food
📢
Report Lost Plasters
Immediately - full investigation required
  • Covering Cuts and Grazes: All cuts and grazes on exposed skin must be covered with waterproof, blue metal detectable plasters provided by the company
  • Authorised Application: Plasters must be applied by an authorised person - never use personal plasters
  • Food Handling Protection: When handling food, company-issued plasters must be covered with a glove
  • Lost Plaster Protocol: Report lost plasters immediately - a full investigation must be conducted to locate them
  • End of Shift Procedure: At the end of your shift, show your plaster to the person who issued it to you, so we can record that it has not been lost or damaged
📋 Remember: Plaster accountability is crucial for food safety - we must track every plaster from issue to return.

📹 CCTV Surveillance

📹 CCTV Monitoring System
🎯 Purpose: CCTV provides a safe environment, protects property, deters crime, and may be used in disciplinary procedures.
  • 24/7 Operation: System operates continuously with respect for privacy
  • GDPR Compliant: Meets all legal requirements and BRC standards
  • Strategic Placement: Cameras throughout the site, not always in obvious locations
  • Limited Access: Only authorised staff can access images
  • Data Retention: Footage retained for maximum 30 days unless required for ongoing investigation
  • Limited Disclosure: Images only disclosed to law enforcement or for valid documented reasons like insurance claims
