Food Safety: Production & Warehouse Guidelines for Foreign Body Control

Ensuring the safety of our honey products is paramount. This presentation outlines essential guidelines for all personnel working in production and warehouse areas, to maintain a safe and hygienic work environment.

Personal Items in Production and Warehouse Areas

Mobile Phones

None company-issued electronic devices are strictly prohibited.

Money & Wallets

No cash or personal financial items allowed.

Jewellery

Only plain band wedding rings are permitted.

Personal Bags

No personal bags in production areas.

Personal Items
Items like phones, money, and personal bags are strictly prohibited in production and warehouse areas.
Jewellery Restrictions
Only a single plain band ring is permitted.
Jewellery Restrictions
Watches, cufflinks, tiepins, and decorative clothing are not permitted.

Other Jewellery

Any jewellery worn for ethnic, medical, or religious reasons must be reported to management for risk assessment and must be covered and strictly controlled if permitted.

Glasses, Contact Lenses & Foreign Body Risk

Lost Contact Lenses

Lost contact lenses, false eyes, or broken spectacles must be reported to management immediately.

Glasses Restrictions

Glasses should not be decorated. Reading glasses should be stored in a hard case when not worn.

Broken Glass

Broken glass should be reported immediately to management. Any broken glass must be disposed of properly and safely.

Pens, Knives & Storage Practices

Pens

Only company-issued blue metal detectable one-piece pens should be used... Pen tops must remain on when not in use.

Storage

Do not store any glass or glass containers in lockers... only coats should be on the hook.

Knives

Only site-issued knives are permitted. No other knives are allowed on site and will be confiscated and destroyed.

This measure helps ensure a safe and controlled environment for all personnel.

Don't use our packaging as storage containers

Under no circumstances should a food container which goes to the customer be used for anything except food.

Understanding the Importance of Food Safety

  • Maintaining Product Integrity: Adhering to these guidelines ensures the highest standards of hygiene and safety.
  • Protecting Consumer Trust: Strict adherence to these rules reinforces our commitment to delivering safe and high-quality honey.
  • Preventing Contamination: It's everyone's responsibility to follow these guidelines to prevent accidental contamination.
